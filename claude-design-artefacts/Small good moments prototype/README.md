# The Small Good Moments

A field guide to the world's gentle wellbeing rituals — culturally specific words for the good little parts of being alive (lingering at the table after a meal, the first beer outdoors after winter, doing nothing at all and meaning it), structured so the app can remind, encourage and prompt you to actually do them.

The app surfaces a ritual suited to the current time of day, lets you browse 52 rituals across five feeling-based clusters (Pause, Outside, Savour, Gather, Reflect), and offers a quiet breathing "moment" timer for the ones you can do right now.

## Running it

The app is a single self-contained HTML file. Open it directly in a browser:

```
open "Small Good Moments.dc.html"
```

`support.js` must sit alongside the `.dc.html` files — it is the lightweight runtime the components render with. No build step or install is required.

## Files

| File | What it is |
|---|---|
| `Small Good Moments.dc.html` | The app. |
| `Small Good Moments - Directions.dc.html` | Visual exploration — four mood directions across mobile, moment mode and desktop. |
| `support.js` | Runtime required by the `.dc.html` files. |
| `uploads/rituals-catalogue.md` | The source catalogue of 52 rituals with full metadata and cultural notes. |

## A note of respect

Several of these words come from living cultures and traditions, some sacred. Entries that touch on sacred practice, commercialisation, or ambivalent meaning carry a "hold this gently" caution and their cultural context in the app. They are shared with care and curiosity, not as decoration.

## Source

Definitions are condensed and rephrased for app use from:
Lomas, T. (2016). *Towards a positive cross-cultural lexicography: Enriching our emotional landscape through 216 'untranslatable' words pertaining to wellbeing.* Journal of Positive Psychology.
